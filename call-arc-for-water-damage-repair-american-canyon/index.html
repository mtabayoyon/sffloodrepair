When Bad Weather Brings the Storm Inside

Torrential rains, strong winds and powerful storms create the conditions necessary for a flood in your home or workplace. Whether you had a leaky roof from the start or you sustained damage from strong winds or rising water, a storm can leave disrepair in its wake. It doesn’t take a broken levy to cause a major flood. Something as small as a window broken by a tree branch can allow rain to leak into your home. Wooden windowsills are particularly prone to dry rot and mold growth after storms.

What Does Flood Damage Repair Entail?

Flood repair after a storm is usually an involved process. Your technician first evaluates the extent of the damage and creates a restoration plan. This may involve the removal of water, dehumidification and property repair. Mold testing is another important step in flood response, and it takes a professional crew to ensure complete recovery. Though accidents happen, and there may be little to stop the flow of water from a nearby lake or river, having your roof inspected once or twice per year can help spot potential leaks or other problems before they become catastrophic.

Leaky and Broken Pipes

Even the best of plumbing jobs wear down over time; this is especially true on older homes. A broken pipe can quickly usher gallons of water into an area and cause further damage throughout the plumbing system. Though the rush of water from plumbing systems may be easier to shut off than water from a storm, the damage can be just as bad, if not potentially worse. With a direct flow of water from a pipe coming into your building, water can build up rapidly, seeping through the walls and creating the conditions for future problems. Water damage from plumbing doesn't always have to be catastrophic, however. A leaky pipe might not be apparent at first; hidden behind the walls, water can continue to drip and create damage, as well as lead to mold growth. A flood damage repair team can both locate and solve small, hidden problems, or assist with major plumbing failure: stopping the rush of water, removing standing floods and performing complete drying services to restore property to its original condition.

Mold Testing and Removal

Water damage isn't the only threat when it comes to a flood. Mold is a pernicious and toxic threat that can occur in almost any wet or moist environment. It can also spread quickly through a building, especially ones with heating, ventilation and air-conditioning units, which carry the spores from room to room. Household cleaners aren't enough to completely deal with mold, and sometimes, finding areas of growth can be close to impossible — especially if they're tucked away in wall cavities and hard-to-reach areas. A professional flood damage repair team inspects and tests a site for mold growth, locates the areas and executes a complete remediation process to clean the area.
