The Dangers of Smoke While fire related hazards are more apparent and understandable, many homeowners make the mistake of assuming that smoke, at worst, leaves only an odor or light discoloration behind. Some homeowners even try to clean smoke stained areas themselves, often making the problem worse. The oils and salts found in human skin react with smoke residue in a way that causes more significant—and even more permanent—property damage. Proper cleanup protocols and techniques are essential. Smoke should be a top concern for homeowners who have fallen victim to a fire. More than mere property damage, smoke poses a health risk; it leaves behind toxic residue that can be inhaled or absorbed into the body from contact, so smoke can be detrimental to the health of all home occupants. Given it’s acidic nature and ability to travel virtually anywhere in the house, smoke can also cause further damage to property over time. Paint and furniture finishes can slowly be eaten away, and electrical components can be damaged, creating conditions for a future fire. While the destruction of fire may leave once the flames have been extinguished, smoke lingers, destroying property, creating conditions for future fires and causing unhealthy conditions. Smoke damage restoration services can effectively clean an area exposed to smoke and restore property that has been damaged. Even if there aren’t any clear indications of smoke damage, it’s usually a smart idea to have an area previously exposed to fire properly checked and cleaned.
