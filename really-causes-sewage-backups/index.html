What Really Causes Sewage Backups? 

Sewage clogs can be a nightmare. The smell, the damage to property and the hassle of finding the source can all cause a headache for property owners. Like most types of property damage, the best fix is prevention, and understanding what leads to sewage backups is the first step toward avoiding them.

Here are the most common causes of sewage backups in San Francisco:

Outside Forces 

Sometimes, even the most responsible efforts aren't enough to combat the causes of a sewage clog. This is especially true in San Francisco. As Mercury News reports, many of the city's sewage pipes are not only old, but they also contain materials that are prone to damage. 

Tree root intrusion, whereby the roots of trees breach and crack sewage lines, is one of the most common causes of clogs, backups and spills. Heavy rains are another common cause of sewage spills. When large amounts of water flood sewer lines, it is not uncommon for the entire system to become overwhelmed. During severe storms, tree branches, leaves, dirt and stones can easily create a clog within a sewage system.

Inside Forces 

Outside of weather and city sewage systems, homeowners actually have a great deal of control when it comes to preventing sewage backups. One of the first and most important steps is knowing what should and should not go into drains and toilets. 

Homeowners should avoid flushing large objects that are not intended to go down the toilet; this can lead to a massive backup. Also, pouring certain substances, such as grease and cooking oil, down the sink drain can create problems. 

Old plumbing and hidden problems in the pipes of a home can create the right conditions for a clog. This is why it is helpful to have a plumber inspect the condition of the house's plumbing systems at least once per year. 

Keep in mind that small problems can worsen up over time, leading to disastrous consequences if they are not addressed. This is why it is important to act quickly when it comes to property damage.
