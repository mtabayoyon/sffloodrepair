Our Mold Inspection Process

  • Visual inspection of suspected mold areas
  • Air sampling to detect and count airborne mold spores
  • Surface sampling and swab testing when indicated
  • Moisture survey using professional meters and thermal imaging
  • Written report of findings with photo documentation
  • Remediation plan and cost estimate

Air Sample Testing

Air sampling is the most effective way to determine the extent of mold contamination in a building. Our technicians collect air samples from affected and unaffected areas, then send them to a certified laboratory for analysis. Results identify the types of mold present and spore counts, which guides the remediation scope.

Moisture Survey

Finding and eliminating the moisture source is essential to successful mold remediation. Without addressing the source, mold will return. Our moisture surveys use calibrated meters, thermal cameras, and physical inspection to map all moisture intrusion points in the structure.

When to Get a Mold Inspection

  • You notice a musty or persistent odor
  • You've had water damage within the last 72 hours
  • You see visible mold growth on walls, ceilings, or floors
  • Someone in your household has unexplained respiratory symptoms
  • You're buying or selling a property
  • Your basement or crawl space has been wet
