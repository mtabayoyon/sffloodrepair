Mold Growth and Its Causes Sometimes it's difficult to pinpoint exactly what leads to mold growth, but water damage and dampness are the two most common culprits. Both attics and basements are prone to significant dampness and moisture. As a result, mold commonly grows in these areas. In fact, mold is so common in them that many people assume the distinctive mold smell is normal in attics and basements. This is a dangerous misconception that can allow mold to stick around longer than it should. Leaky pipes are a common cause of mold because they can cause water damage that is often behind walls or in ceilings. When it’s in a hard-to-see spot, mold can grow undisturbed until it has become rampant throughout the entire building. A major flood or other source of massive water damage also presents a prime opportunity for mold to develop and spread. In these situations, water damage can penetrate deep into various parts of a building, not only causing mold to grow in unexpected areas but also in large quantities. The countless causes of mold are one reason why it's so critical to trust a mold inspection team to find and locate active sites. While you may be able to find some areas on your own, a professional service can find all the damage in your home and immediately get to work using industry standards to clean the area. Mold Remediation Services Mold testing and remediation go hand-in-hand: After locating all affected areas, a professional team can apply effective mold cleaning solutions and eliminate the chance of excessive exposure or contamination in other areas. Without proper remediation techniques, it is highly likely that mold will resurface. A professional team can determine the initial cause of the growth and put a stop to that, as well. Mold and Health Risks For homeowners, mold presents a serious threat to the health and safety of their families. For business owners, mold can create a toxic environment for both workers and customers. Even if the problem seems small, getting rid of active mold sites should be a priority when it comes to maintaining a safe and healthy environment. Mold reproduces by releasing spores into the air, and anyone within the vicinity can inhale them easily. While mold allergies are fairly common, excessive exposure can cause reactions in almost anyone. Asthmatics, the elderly and children are particularly at risk of developing breathing complications in the presence of mold. Since mold spores spread so easily, they are often on various surfaces throughout a building. Doorknobs, chairs, tables and other items can all become resting areas for mold spores. While these may not lead to active sites, they can cause contact with human skin and eyes, leading to irritation and rashes.
