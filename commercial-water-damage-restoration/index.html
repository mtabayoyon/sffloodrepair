Commercial Water Damage Response Process

Rapid Mobilization

Multiple crews dispatched simultaneously for large-loss commercial events. Allied Restoration maintains a commercial equipment fleet including high-capacity truck-mounted extractors, desiccant dehumidifiers, and large-format air movers capable of handling warehouse-scale drying.

Moisture Mapping

Full building moisture assessment using thermal imaging cameras and calibrated moisture meters. All affected areas documented with floor-by-floor mapping — critical for complex multi-tenant commercial buildings where water travel paths are difficult to trace.

Commercial Extraction

High-capacity extraction equipment removes standing water from large surface areas quickly. Specialized floor extraction tools for commercial carpet and hard surface flooring. Elevator pit and sub-basement pumping capability.

Desiccant Drying Systems

Commercial desiccant dehumidifiers process thousands of cubic feet of air per minute — essential for large commercial spaces where LGR units are insufficient. Allied Rental Company provides a full commercial drying equipment fleet.

Business Continuity Planning

We work around your operational schedule wherever possible — nights and weekends for occupied retail and office buildings, phased drying to allow continued partial occupancy, and rapid-track timelines when revenue loss is occurring.

Commercial Reconstruction

Full commercial reconstruction including tenant improvement rebuilds, ADA compliance, commercial flooring, and HVAC system repairs. Licensed commercial contractor with experience in San Francisco permitting requirements.

Common Commercial Water Damage Sources

  • Rooftop HVAC units and cooling towers — condensation line failures and pan overflows are the leading cause of commercial water damage in SF high-rises
  • Sprinkler system failures — accidental activation, corrosion, and freeze events in building mechanical rooms
  • Upper-floor plumbing failures — cascading damage through multiple floors and tenant spaces in multi-story buildings
  • Roof membrane failures — flat roofs common in commercial San Francisco construction require regular maintenance
  • Basement and parking garage flooding — storm drain backup and sump pump failures
  • Restaurant kitchen and bathroom floods — commercial kitchen equipment and high-use restrooms in retail and hospitality properties
