1. Call Flood-Remediation Specialists

Timely response to a flood at home means the difference between restoring property and losing it, according to Home Advisor. Water damage increases over time and also allows for other threats, like mold growth, to occur. If the flood is associated at all with raw sewage or other contaminants, other health hazards come into play.

2. Maintaining Cleanliness With Rubber Boots, Gloves and Other Materials

Floods not only cause damage to property, but under the right conditions, they can be hazardous to health and safety. Many homeowners rightfully associate water damage with mold growth, but that’s not all; dirty, contaminated water can bring with it bacteria and pathogens that can be easily spread throughout the home.

According to the CDC, purchasing quality rubber boots, waterproof gloves, breathing masks, mops and cleaning supplies should be a priority when reacting to a flood. Boots, gloves and masks should only be worn in areas close to the flood in order to minimize bacteria or pathogens spreading throughout the house. Areas surrounding the flood should also be kept clean with mops and other cleaning materials.

3. Contact Professional Services

Floods are much more than just “a little bit of water;” they are often the causes of major property destruction and expensive repairs. After a remediation company has been contacted and is on the way, and all other preparations have been taken care of, be sure to contact not only the insurance company but plumbing, electric or roofing services—depending on what else needs to be fixed. Remember: Floods often result from an issue present in the home, and repairing that is just as important as responding to the water damage.
