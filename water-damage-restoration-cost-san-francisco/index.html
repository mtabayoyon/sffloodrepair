What Affects Water Damage Restoration Cost in San Francisco

  • Water category — clean water (Category 1) is cheapest. Grey water (Category 2) requires antimicrobials. Sewage (Category 3) requires full biohazard protocols and costs significantly more.
  • Square footage affected — water travels fast. A burst pipe can soak multiple rooms before it's shut off. The more area affected, the more drying equipment and time required.
  • Building materials — concrete takes 2–3x longer to dry than drywall. Hardwood floors require specialty mat systems. Dense insulation must often be removed entirely.
  • Access and parking — SF Victorian homes with narrow access, high-rise buildings with elevator-only entry, and street parking limitations add labor time and cost in San Francisco specifically.
  • How quickly you call — every hour of delay allows water to spread and materials to absorb more moisture. A 2-hour delay can double the scope. Calling Allied immediately minimizes total cost.
  • Reconstruction needed — mitigation (drying) and reconstruction (rebuilding) are separate phases. Reconstruction cost depends entirely on what was damaged.

Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Water Damage Company

  • Are your technicians IICRC WRT and ASD certified? (Ask to see the certifications)
  • Do you use Xactimate for documentation? (Required by most insurance carriers)
  • Do you have a physical Bay Area office — not just a dispatch center?
  • What is your guaranteed response time for emergencies?
  • Will you coordinate directly with my insurance adjuster?
  • Do you provide daily moisture monitoring logs?
  • Do you handle reconstruction as well as mitigation?

Red Flags to Avoid

  • Contractors who demand full payment upfront before work begins
  • Contractors without IICRC certification or who can't verify it
  • Extremely low quotes — often indicates scope is being missed
  • No written estimate before work starts
  • No moisture monitoring logs — required for valid insurance claims
  • Contractors who discourage you from involving your insurance carrier
