Smoke damage is deceptive. While fire destroys what it directly contacts, smoke silently penetrates every corner of a structure — depositing acidic soot on surfaces, infiltrating HVAC systems, and embedding odor compounds deep into porous materials. Smoke damage repair requires specialized equipment and techniques that most general contractors simply don't have. Allied Restoration's IICRC FSRT-certified technicians have handled hundreds of smoke damage projects across the San Francisco Bay Area.

Why Smoke Damage Is Worse Than It Looks

Smoke damage is not limited to burned or visibly blackened areas. As smoke travels through a structure:

  • Acidic soot begins etching metal, glass, and plastic surfaces within hours — causing permanent damage if not treated immediately
  • Smoke travels through HVAC systems and deposits residue throughout every room, including rooms far from the fire
  • Smoke odor penetrates wall cavities, insulation, furniture, and clothing — standard cleaning products cannot reach these deposits
  • Protein residue from kitchen fires sears onto surfaces as a nearly invisible yellowish film that is notoriously difficult to remove
  • Unspent combustible material in smoke residue creates a risk of secondary ignition

Our Smoke Damage Repair Process

1. Emergency Stabilization

Board-up and tarping to secure the structure. Full soot mapping throughout every room using specialized testing. Xactimate documentation begun immediately for insurance adjuster.

2. Soot Removal

Dry-ice blasting removes soot from structural surfaces — including wood framing, concrete, and brick — without chemicals or moisture. HEPA vacuuming removes fine particulates from all surfaces.

3. Chemical Cleaning

Specialized soot cleaning agents for different surface types — dry-side cleaning sponges for painted surfaces, chemical cleaners for hard surfaces, ultrasonic cleaning for contents and personal items.

4. Odor Elimination

Hydroxyl generators break down smoke odor compounds at the molecular level — safe to operate while occupants are present. Thermal fogging penetrates the same pathways smoke traveled. Ozone treatment for severe cases.

5. Water Damage

Fire sprinkler and firefighting water extracted and dried. Mold prevention treatment applied. Concurrent water restoration to prevent secondary mold growth during smoke remediation.

6. Reconstruction

Xactimate estimate submitted to insurance. Rebuild to pre-loss condition — framing, drywall, flooring, cabinetry, and finish work. One company handles everything from cleanup through rebuild.

Types of Smoke Damage We Treat

  • Structural fire smoke damage — residential and commercial structure fires
  • Kitchen fire damage — protein fires from cooking leave a distinctive greasy residue requiring specialized treatment
  • Wildfire smoke infiltration — smoke from distant wildfires enters through HVAC systems and building gaps
  • Cannabis and tobacco smoke — chronic exposure creates embedded odor requiring professional deodorization
  • Electrical fire smoke — highly corrosive residue that requires immediate treatment
  • Vehicle fire damage — garage fires often damage the attached structure through smoke infiltration

Will Smoke Smell Go Away on Its Own?

No — smoke odor does not dissipate on its own over time. Smoke particles that have penetrated building materials will continue to off-gas for months or even years without professional treatment. Opening windows and using air fresheners temporarily masks the odor but does not remove the embedded compounds. Allied Restoration's combination of thermal fogging and hydroxyl generation reaches the same pathways the smoke traveled and neutralizes the odor compounds at a molecular level — permanently eliminating the smell rather than covering it.
