Even the Smallest Fires Pose a Risk

Whether from a faulty electrical outlet, improper disposal of matches, cigarette butts, or any number of other causes, some fires remain in a single area or room. While the remainder of the home may seem safe, there are other risks — structural damage, or the chance for another fire. Homeowners may not even be aware of these dangers until fire restoration services are called in to survey and repair the area.

The Dangers of Smoke

While fire-related hazards are more apparent, many homeowners make the mistake of assuming that smoke, at worst, leaves only an odor behind. Some homeowners even try to clean smoke-stained areas themselves, often making the problem worse.

Smoke leaves behind toxic residue that can be inhaled or absorbed into the body from contact, making it detrimental to the health of all home occupants. Given its acidic nature and ability to travel virtually anywhere in the house, smoke can also cause further damage to property over time — paint and furniture finishes can be slowly eaten away, and electrical components can be damaged, creating conditions for a future fire.
