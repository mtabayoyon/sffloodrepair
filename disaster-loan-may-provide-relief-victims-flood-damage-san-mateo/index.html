Record-Breaking Storms Hit San Francisco 

Few are likely to forget 2014's exit with the wave of violent weather that hit the Bay Area. With unprecedented rains, strong winds and even snow, highways were shut down while home- and business owners suffered immense property damage. Downed trees and rushing floods caused destruction to buildings, homes and vehicles.

Although the city is still in recovery mode, many concerned citizens and business owners are grateful that the SBA will deliver assistance.

Moving on after Disaster 

There is more involved in disaster recovery than just repair expenses. For companies, one of the most critical elements in disaster recovery is being able to open shop as soon as possible. This is especially important in San Francisco where real estate costs are steep. Depending on the level of destruction, restoring a business may take several weeks. 

The recent weather in San Francisco has reminded may business owners how important it is to have a disaster recovery plan. A fast response can shorten the recovery time and may be able to save you money. 

For homeowners, property damage may render their houses unlivable. For some, this can be particularly worrisome if there are no other places to go while the repairs take place. It is always best, especially for families, to work out a support network of friends and family who can provide shelter if the worst happens.
