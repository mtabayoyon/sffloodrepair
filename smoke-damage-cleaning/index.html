Smoke Damage Cleaning & Restoration

Smoke damage extends far beyond what a fire touches. Smoke and soot travel throughout a building, embedding in surfaces, contents, and the HVAC system — and the acidic residue keeps causing damage long after the fire is out. Allied Restoration provides professional smoke damage cleaning across the Bay Area, removing soot and neutralizing odor at the source rather than masking it.

The Smoke Damage Cleaning Process

  • Assessment — identifying the type and extent of smoke and soot damage
  • Surface cleaning — removing acidic soot from all affected surfaces before it causes permanent etching
  • Contents cleaning — restoring salvageable belongings, textiles, and electronics
  • HVAC decontamination — cleaning the ductwork that circulated smoke, so it does not re-contaminate
  • Odor neutralization — thermal fogging, hydroxyl, and ozone treatment to destroy odor at the source
  • Sealing — locking in any residual odor before restoration

Why Professional Smoke Cleaning Matters

DIY smoke cleaning usually fails because smoke penetrates deep into porous materials and the HVAC system, where surface cleaning cannot reach. Odor returns on warm, humid days because the source was never removed. Professional smoke restoration addresses the source, the structure, and the air together.
