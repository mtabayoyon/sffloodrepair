If you have experienced water damage in the past, then you probably understand how likely the threat of mold is after such an occurrence. Both small leaks and catastrophic floods can create the moist conditions necessary for mold to grow. Mold can also form in hard-to-reach areas after water damage, such as dry wall cavities, under floors and above ceilings. Mold tends to be more common in attics and basements, but excess moisture and dampness can cause mold to form on just about any surface, especially carpeting, furniture, clothing, books, paper and more. Mold will rarely stay in one area. Active sites continuously release spores into the air, which travel in search of new areas to land and grow. Mold in the attic can easily spread through the HVAC system and land in kitchens and bathrooms. Building inhabitants can inhale these spores, which can lead to respiratory illnesses and allergic reactions. Give the fact that mold can spread so easily throughout a building, scheduling a professional mold inspection is the only guaranteed way to locate every active site. Without the support of an experienced restoration team, a small site may go overlooked and continue spreading throughout a home. Mold Causes Respiratory Illnesses and Allergic Reactions Perhaps one of the most immediate reasons to consult a mold remediation company is the threat mold poses to the health of building inhabitants. Mold constantly releases spores into the air, and if they travel through the air duct system, they threaten the air quality in every room. Many people are allergic to mold, and spores can trigger violent asthma attacks and cause serious respiratory issues for the elderly and infants. Breathing complications and frequent allergic reactions commons sign of a mold issue – even if there are no visibly active sites. Mold can grow on almost any surface of a building, including chairs, doorknobs, TV remotes, computers and phones. People regularly come into contact with many of these surfaces, which can cause skin irritations and other medical problems.
