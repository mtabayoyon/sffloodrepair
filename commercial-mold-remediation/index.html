Commercial mold in a San Francisco Bay Area property creates liability, health risks for occupants, and potential regulatory consequences. Landlords, property managers, HOAs, and commercial building owners face significant exposure if mold is not properly documented, remediated, and prevented. Allied Restoration's IICRC AMRT-certified technicians provide comprehensive commercial mold services — from initial inspection through clearance certification — with the documentation required to satisfy insurance carriers, building inspectors, and legal counsel.

Commercial Mold Services

Commercial Mold Inspection

Full building visual inspection, moisture survey, and air sampling. HVAC system assessment. Written report with lab results, photographic documentation, and remediation scope recommendations — suitable for insurance, legal, and regulatory use.

Large-Scale Remediation

Commercial-scale containment, negative air pressure systems, and HEPA air scrubbing for large remediation projects. Multiple crew capacity for multi-floor or multi-unit remediation. Phased approach to allow partial building occupancy where possible.

HVAC Mold Treatment

Commercial HVAC systems are a primary mold distribution pathway. Allied Restoration treats and cleans ductwork, air handlers, and coils — preventing remediated spaces from being re-contaminated through the ventilation system.

Multi-Unit Coordination

In apartment buildings and condominiums, mold in one unit frequently affects adjacent units through shared wall cavities and plumbing chases. Allied coordinates multi-unit remediation with separate documentation for each affected space — critical for insurance claims and HOA disputes.

Clearance Certification

Post-remediation air sampling by independent industrial hygienist. Written clearance report confirming mold levels are within regulatory limits. Required for insurance claims, landlord-tenant disputes, and commercial real estate transactions.

Reconstruction & Prevention

Rebuild remediated areas with appropriate moisture barriers and ventilation improvements to prevent recurrence. Identify and repair moisture sources — roof leaks, plumbing failures, HVAC condensation issues — as part of the complete remediation scope.

California Commercial Mold Regulations

California has specific regulations around commercial mold disclosure and remediation that property owners and managers must comply with:

  • California Health & Safety Code Section 17920.3 — defines visible mold as a substandard building condition requiring remediation
  • AB 284 — requires landlords to disclose known mold conditions to tenants
  • OSHA standards — commercial employers have obligations regarding mold in the workplace
  • Allied Restoration provides documentation suitable for regulatory compliance, legal proceedings, and real estate disclosure requirements

Why SF Bay Area Commercial Properties Are High-Risk for Mold

  • Year-round coastal fog elevates ambient humidity across the entire Bay Area
  • Older commercial building stock (pre-1980) often lacks adequate vapor barriers and building envelope sealing
  • Flat roofs common in commercial SF construction are prone to ponding and membrane failures
  • Dense urban construction limits natural ventilation and sunlight penetration
  • Complex HVAC systems in multi-story buildings create multiple condensation risk points
