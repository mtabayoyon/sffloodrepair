Do not attempt to clean smoke or soot yourself, as improper cleaning can permanently set stains and spread contamination.
Smoke damage is significantly more dangerous than most homeowners realize. Smoke residue contains hundreds of toxic compounds, many of which can be absorbed through the skin or inhaled. The residue is acidic and will continue to etch and damage surfaces — metals, fabrics, electronics, and finishes — for days and weeks after the fire if not properly cleaned. Professional smoke restoration is not just about odor removal; it's about decontamination and preventing ongoing damage.
Yes — fire and smoke damage is typically one of the most universally covered perils under standard homeowner's insurance policies. This generally includes the cost of emergency board-up, contents cleaning, structural restoration, temporary housing, and reconstruction. Allied Restoration works directly with all major insurance carriers and uses Xactimate to ensure your claim is documented and submitted correctly.
Emergency stabilization (board-up, tarping, initial cleaning) happens within the first 24–72 hours. Full smoke and soot removal from surfaces, contents, and HVAC systems typically takes 3–7 days for a moderate residential fire. Reconstruction — replacing drywall, flooring, cabinets, and other damaged structural components — varies widely based on the extent of the fire but typically takes 2–8 weeks.
Soot is the black, powdery residue left by incomplete combustion. It is highly acidic, contains toxic compounds, and has an extremely fine particle size that allows it to penetrate porous surfaces and travel through HVAC systems throughout the building. Improper cleaning — wiping or scrubbing with regular household cleaners — typically spreads and embeds soot deeper into surfaces rather than removing it. Professional cleaning uses specific techniques and products designed for each surface type to safely remove soot without further damage.
Yes, but it requires professional techniques. Surface cleaning alone rarely eliminates smoke odor because the odor-causing compounds penetrate deep into porous materials — drywall, insulation, wood framing, carpets, and soft goods. Allied Restoration uses ozone treatment, hydroxyl generation, and thermal fogging to penetrate and neutralize odor compounds throughout the structure. In severe cases, affected porous materials may need to be removed and replaced.
