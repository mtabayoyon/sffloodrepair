Finding Out If Your Business Is in a High-Risk Area for Flooding

While every business or home can benefit from simple flood-prevention methods, understanding how much is necessary is all about knowing whether or not the location is considered a high-risk area for floods. According to Floodsmart.gov, high-risk areas often benefit from more flood insurance options.

Next Steps

As Disastersafety.org reports, knowing how frequently an area may experience floods will also indicate how much preparation should go into keeping property and stock safe from water damage.

Limit What’s in the Basement

Basements suffer the most during floods. For this reason, it’s a smart idea to find ways to limit reliance on basement storage. Digitizing important financial documents is one way to cut down on physical damage. These files should also be backed up and stored somewhere off site as computers can easily be destroyed from a flood.

Keeping important materials and property elevated often prevents the chances of extreme loss. It is also wise to hire an electrician to flood proof wiring, outlets and other areas where water can cause further problems.
