A large-loss event — a flooded warehouse, a fire across multiple floors, a burst main in a high-rise — demands capabilities most restoration companies simply don't have. Allied Restoration's large-loss division brings the equipment fleet, crew capacity, and project management that catastrophic commercial and industrial losses require.

What qualifies as a large loss? Generally any commercial or industrial damage requiring extensive equipment, multiple crews, phased project management, or specialized drying of large structures. If it's beyond the scale of a typical job, it's a large loss — and it's what we're built for.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is large-loss restoration?
Large-loss restoration refers to major commercial or industrial damage requiring extensive equipment, multiple crews, and phased project management — such as flooded warehouses, multi-floor fires, or high-rise water damage. It demands capabilities beyond a typical restoration job.
What equipment is needed for large-loss commercial drying?
Large losses require desiccant dehumidification for big open structures, three-phase temporary power when building power is compromised, high-volume extraction, and large fleets of LGR dehumidifiers and air movers — all of which Allied maintains.
How does Allied minimize downtime in a large loss?
We deploy equipment at scale immediately, dry to IICRC standard quickly, sequence work to reopen critical areas first, and provide dedicated project management and daily documentation to keep the recovery and the insurance claim moving.
