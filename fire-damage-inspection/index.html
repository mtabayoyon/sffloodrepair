After a fire, the damage you can see is only part of the story. Smoke travels into wall cavities, soot settles in HVAC systems, and water used to extinguish the fire soaks into the structure. A professional fire damage inspection documents all of it — visible and hidden — to create an accurate restoration plan and a complete insurance claim.

What Happens During a Fire Damage Inspection

  1. Safety assessment. Before anything else, the inspector confirms the structure is safe to enter — checking for structural compromise, electrical hazards, and air quality concerns.
  2. Structural evaluation. Assessing damage to framing, walls, ceilings, floors, and the roof to determine what can be restored and what must be replaced.
  3. Smoke and soot mapping. Documenting how far smoke and soot traveled — often into rooms far from the fire, into HVAC systems, and inside wall cavities.
  4. Water damage assessment. Firefighting introduces large volumes of water. The inspector uses moisture meters and thermal imaging to map water damage that will need drying.
  5. Contents evaluation. Determining which belongings can be restored (cleaned, deodorized) versus those that are a total loss.
  6. Documentation for insurance. Photographs, moisture readings, and a detailed Xactimate scope that your insurance adjuster requires.

Why You Need a Professional Fire Damage Inspection

Insurance companies require detailed documentation to process a fire claim — and the scope they approve is based on what's documented. An incomplete inspection means an incomplete claim, leaving you to pay out of pocket for damage that should have been covered. Frequently Asked Questions

What does a fire damage inspection include?
It includes a safety assessment, structural evaluation, smoke and soot mapping, water damage assessment from firefighting, contents evaluation, and complete documentation for your insurance claim including photos and a Xactimate scope.
How soon after a fire should I get an inspection?
As soon as the fire department clears the property as safe to enter. Fast inspection and response limits secondary damage from smoke, soot, and water, and gets your insurance claim moving quickly.
