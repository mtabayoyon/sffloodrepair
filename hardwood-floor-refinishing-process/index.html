What Goes into the Wood Floor Refinishing Process?

After scheduling a visit from a contractor from Allied Restoration Company, a professional will arrive at your home or business to assess the current condition of the wood flooring. The contractor will also discuss your goals and provide recommendations. Then, we will provide a quote and create a plan to complete your project. In order to make the process easier, it is best to remove all furniture from the area so the contractors can be as productive as possible. The team will start by cleaning the existing floor, removing any dirt, dust or other contaminants that may harm the wood underneath. The next step will involve stripping or sanding away what's left of the original finish. With the bare wood exposed, our team will perform any necessary repairs, such as fixing cracks, holes or replacing specific floorboards. Prior to applying the new finish, an industrial buffer will completely remove whatever is left of the original finish. After dry-tacking the floor, the contractors will apply the new finish.

Care and Maintenance for Recently Refinished Hardwood Floors

Seeing a newly refinished floor is a satisfying experienced for home- and business owners, and you can make that feeling last with proper care. This will prolong the life of the floor and the new finish. It is important to follow these steps for newly refinished floors:
  • Avoid walking on the floor for up to 48 hours.
  • Wait up to one month to place furniture in the room.
  • Try to keep the area dirt and dust free for up to one month.
  • Avoid spilling any water or liquids on the floor for up to two months.
These guidelines will help home- and business owners get the most out of their new floors. It is also important to ask the contractors for maintenance advice. There may be special considerations based on the building’s construction, finish or stain choices, location of the floor, and other factors. To maximize the lifespan of the new finish, place felt pads under furniture legs. Homeowners should also consider only walking barefoot or with socks on the floor. This will decrease the likelihood of leaving scuffmarks and scratches.
