The Crawl Space & Foundation Moisture Guide

What happens in the dark gap under your floor doesn't stay there. How crawl space moisture becomes a mold, air-quality, and structural problem for the whole house — and how it's permanently solved.

Read7 min
UpdatedJune 2026
ByAllied Restoration
ForHomeowners · Managers · Inspectors

The crawl space is the most ignored part of a building and the source of some of its most expensive problems. What happens in that dark gap under the floor — moisture, mold, wood rot, poor air quality — doesn't stay there. It rises into the living space above through a process most owners have never heard of.

SECTION 01The Stack Effect: Why Crawl Space Air Becomes Your Air

Here's the counterintuitive truth that makes crawl spaces matter: air moves upward through a building. Warm air rises and escapes through the upper floors, which pulls air up from below to replace it — drawing crawl space air up into your living space. Building scientists call this the stack effect, and studies suggest a large share of the air on your ground floor originated in the crawl space below.

FIG 01 — The stack effect: as warm air rises and exits upper floors, it draws crawl space air — and whatever is in it — up into the living space.

This is why a moldy, damp crawl space is not just a structural concern — it's an indoor air quality problem for the people living above it.

SECTION 02Why Bay Area Crawl Spaces Are Especially Vulnerable

  • Coastal humidity and fog keep ground moisture high much of the year
  • Older homes (a third of Bay Area properties predate 1950) often have unsealed, vented crawl spaces and original drainage
  • Hillside lots channel groundwater toward and under foundations
  • Rainy-season saturation from December through February floods low crawl spaces

SECTION 04The Fix: Encapsulation and Drying

Professional crawl space remediation goes beyond removing visible mold — it addresses the moisture that caused it. The modern standard is a sequence:

  • Remove contaminated insulation and standing water
  • Remediate mold under containment and treat structural wood
  • Dry the space fully with commercial dehumidification
  • Encapsulate — seal the space with a vapor barrier to block ground moisture permanently
  • Control humidity ongoing, often with a dedicated crawl space dehumidifier
