Spring Mold: The Hidden Cost of Winter Rain

Spring brings a hidden consequence of winter rain: mold. The moisture that soaked into buildings now has warmth to fuel growth. Spring is mold season — and the time to catch it.

Read6 min
UpdatedJune 2026

Spring in the Bay Area brings a hidden consequence of the winter rains: mold. The moisture that soaked into buildings during months of rain — into crawl spaces, wall cavities, and under floors — now has warming temperatures to fuel growth. Spring is mold's season, and the time to catch it before it spreads.

SECTION 01Why Mold Surfaces in Spring

Mold needs moisture, organic material, and favorable temperatures. Winter provides the moisture through sustained rain and humidity; spring provides the warmth. The result is that water intrusion which seemed minor or even went unnoticed during winter blooms into visible, spreading mold as temperatures rise. Crawl spaces that flooded, walls that got wet, and rooms that stayed humid all become mold sites in spring.

SECTION 02Where to Look First

  • Crawl spaces and basements — where rainy-season groundwater collected and lingers
  • Around windows and exterior walls — where wind-driven rain penetrated
  • Under and around any winter leak — even one that was wiped up but not professionally dried
  • Bathrooms and laundry rooms — chronically humid spaces
  • Behind furniture on exterior walls — where air does not circulate

SECTION 03The Spring Mold Check

  1. Use your nose — walk the property looking (and smelling) for musty odors, the earliest sign
  2. Inspect winter trouble spots — anywhere that got wet over the rainy season
  3. Check the crawl space — the most common and most overlooked mold site
  4. Look for visual signs — discoloration, staining, warping on walls and floors
  5. Monitor humidity — sustained high indoor humidity feeds mold; address ventilation
  6. Get a professional assessment for any past water damage that was not professionally dried
The Post-Winter Window

Spring is when winter's hidden moisture becomes visible mold. If your property saw any water intrusion over the rainy season — even minor — a spring inspection catches developing mold before it spreads. (415) 529-5637.

SECTION 04Why DIY Mold Removal Often Backfires

Finding mold is good; removing it incorrectly can make things worse. Disturbing mold without containment releases spores throughout the building, spreading the contamination. Bleach does not remediate mold on porous surfaces — it removes the color while leaving the root structure. Professional remediation isolates the area, removes affected materials properly, and — most importantly — fixes the moisture source so it does not return. For anything beyond a small surface spot, professional remediation protects both your health and your building.
