Storm Flood Relief and Damage Restoration

Storms are one of the leading causes of floods. Heavy rainstorms are often accompanied by strong winds and other natural forces that can weaken areas of a building, allowing rain to enter and ruin property. Existing leaks and other structural problems can also become worse during periods of frequent rain showers. With time, these issues can lead to serious water damage or a major flood. Storms can also cause local rivers and lakes to overflow, sending water into the basements of nearby buildings. In cases like this, it can be incredibly difficult to stop the flow of water, making it all the more important to contact a professional service as soon as a problem develops. Floods associated with storms typically come with a number of other issues as the water is rarely clean. Mud, dirt and other debris from outside the building can be forced inside during a torrential storm. This type of flood not only contaminates property, but can also accelerate the damage from water. In these cases, only a trained restoration professional is capable of halting and reversing the damage. It is recommended to have your building’s roofing materials, as well as siding, professionally checked at least once or twice a year in order to spot any small issues before they become major problems.

Floods from Plumbing Failures

A building’s plumbing system is susceptible to damage and wear over time; if it isn’t maintained, pipes can easily rupture or break. Sometimes even a perfectly functioning plumbing system can encounter a problem from a clog, or even a torrential downpour outside as the rush of rain water overwhelms a nearby public system. All of these can result in countless gallons of water overflowing and spilling out into your building. While this water may be cleaner than liquid from outside, it has the potential of building up much faster and spreading to other areas more easily. New or old, all plumbing systems are at risk of a catastrophic failure under the right conditions. Sometimes a small leak can contribute to a massive issue later on; one helpful way to prevent this from happening is to have your plumbing examined at least once or twice a year.
