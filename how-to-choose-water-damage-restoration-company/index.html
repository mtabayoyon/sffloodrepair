How to Choose a Water Damage Restoration Company San Francisco

Choosing the wrong water damage restoration company is an expensive mistake. Bad documentation jeopardizes your insurance claim. Uncertified drying leaves hidden moisture that turns into mold. And some contractors use the emergency to overcharge. This guide gives you a clear checklist — verified against IICRC standards and California contractor law — to evaluate any restoration company before you let them work on your property.

The 10-Point Restoration Contractor Checklist

1. IICRC Certification

Ask for the technician's IICRC WRT (Water Restoration Technician) and ASD (Applied Structural Drying) card. Verify at iicrc.org. IICRC certified = trained to industry standard. Uncertified = no standard at all.

2. California Contractor License

Verify at cslb.ca.gov. The license must be active and in good standing. Ask for the license number and verify it yourself — takes 60 seconds. Allied Restoration license: #969355.

3. Xactimate Capability

All major insurance carriers require Xactimate documentation to process claims. If a contractor can't produce a Xactimate estimate, your insurance company may reject their work documentation and deny your claim.

4. Physical Local Office

Verify they have a real office in the Bay Area — not just a Google listing with a call center elsewhere. Ask for the address and look it up. Local companies are accountable. Dispatch-only operations are not.

5. Written Estimate Before Work

Never allow work to begin without a written scope and estimate. Verbal estimates don't hold up in insurance claims or disputes. A legitimate contractor provides written documentation before starting.

6. Daily Moisture Monitoring

IICRC S500 standard requires daily moisture readings throughout the drying period. Ask if they provide daily logs. Without logs, insurance carriers can deny the drying claim and you have no proof the structure was dried to standard.

7. Insurance Carrier Experience

Ask which carriers they work with regularly. A contractor familiar with State Farm, Allstate, Farmers, and USAA processes gets your claim approved faster and with less friction than one who rarely deals with insurance.

8. Thermal Imaging Equipment

A FLIR or similar thermal camera identifies hidden moisture in walls, ceilings, and floors that moisture meters can't reach. Without thermal imaging, moisture gets missed — and that missed moisture grows mold in 24–72 hours.

9. Commercial Drying Equipment

LGR dehumidifiers and high-velocity air movers are the professional standard. Contractors using household dehumidifiers from Home Depot are not equipped to dry structures properly. Ask what drying equipment they use.

10. Full-Service Capability

Some companies only do mitigation (drying) — not reconstruction. Then you need a second contractor for the rebuild. A single company handling both is more efficient, faster, and protects you from scope disputes between contractors.

Red Flags — Walk Away If You See These

  • Demands full payment upfront before any work is done
  • Can't provide IICRC certification numbers for technicians
  • No California contractor license or license is expired/suspended
  • Refuses to provide written estimate before starting work
  • Discourages you from contacting your insurance company
  • Uses household fans and consumer dehumidifiers instead of commercial equipment
  • Quote is dramatically lower than all others — usually means work will be incomplete
  • No physical Bay Area office — just a phone number and website
  • Won't give you their license number to verify
