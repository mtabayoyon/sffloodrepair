Our Commercial Drying Fleet

Desiccant Dehumidification

For large open structures — warehouses, gymnasiums, manufacturing floors — and cold or low-humidity environments, refrigerant (LGR) dehumidifiers can't keep pace. Desiccant systems pull moisture from the air using adsorption rather than condensation, drying large volumes of air fast regardless of temperature. This is essential equipment for true large-loss work, and most residential-focused companies don't own it.

Three-Phase Temporary Power

When a commercial building loses power, or when the existing service can't run the volume of equipment a large dry-out requires, recovery stalls. Allied deploys generators and three-phase power distribution to run high-capacity equipment independent of building power — keeping the recovery moving from hour one.

High-Volume Water Extraction

Truck-mounted and portable extraction units remove large volumes of standing water quickly across big floorplates — the critical first step that limits how far water spreads and how much it damages.

LGR Dehumidifiers & Air Movers

For the structural drying phase, Allied fields a large fleet of low-grain refrigerant dehumidifiers and high-velocity air movers — enough to saturate a large site with equipment and dry it to IICRC S500 standard under continuous monitoring.

Thermal Imaging & Moisture Monitoring

FLIR thermal cameras and moisture meters map hidden water inside walls, ceilings, and structural cavities — ensuring nothing is missed and providing the documented daily readings commercial insurance claims require.

Frequently Asked Questions

What equipment does commercial water damage drying require?
Large commercial losses require desiccant dehumidification for big open spaces, three-phase temporary power, high-volume extraction units, large fleets of LGR dehumidifiers and air movers, and thermal imaging for moisture detection. Allied owns and maintains all of this.
What is desiccant dehumidification and when is it needed?
Desiccant dehumidification removes moisture through adsorption rather than condensation, making it effective in large open structures and cold or low-humidity environments where standard refrigerant dehumidifiers can't keep up. It's essential for warehouse and industrial-scale drying.
