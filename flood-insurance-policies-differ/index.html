Is Flood Insurance Necessary?

The answer to this question largely depends on how prone a particular area is to flooding. What’s important to keep in mind is that flood insurance not only covers water extraction; it also helps pay for issues related to floods such as dry rot and mold.

Next Steps

Floods in the home can potentially cause greater stress on plumbing systems in the house. It’s not unheard of for pipes to weaken—and possibly even rust or burst—as a result of a serious flood. Additionally, the flood may cause sewage backups and clogs, especially if groundwater is a contributing factor.

What Makes Flood Insurance Different?

Sometimes, homeowners aren’t fully aware of what their insurance plan does or does not cover. Depending on the money a homeowner has available, he or she may settle for a plan that could ignore some critical areas.

To be fair, it is not always necessary to get every possible form of coverage. Flood insurance, however, is one that should never be overlooked.

As Houselogic.com suggests, insurance policies concerning floods can be somewhat confusing. Many insurance companies will cover water damage when it comes from rain falling directly into the house; however, if water from an overflowing river or flooded street enters the home, a typical insurance policy may not cover the damage.
