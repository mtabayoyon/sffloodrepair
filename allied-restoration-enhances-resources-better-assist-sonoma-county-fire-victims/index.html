Allied Restoration Enhances Resources to Better Assist Sonoma County Fire Victims

Insurance Coverage

NOVATO, Calif., (April 5, 2018) — Allied Restoration Company has augmented its staff, equipment and other resources to be better able to help victims of the Sonoma County and Santa Rosa fires. The company, headquartered in Navato, Calif., has added additional field technicians and estimators as well as expanded its temporary power and other disaster-recovery equipment to meet the needs of clients recovering from the wildfires that ravaged the area in October 2017. Allied Restoration—which also has offices in Sonoma County and Marin County—has been diligently working to address the demands of the community in the aftermath of the natural disaster. “We have been actively involved with fire and smoke cleanup projects,” says Allied Restoration CEO Michael Tabayoyon. “We have been working with homeowners, commercial property managers and their insurance carriers in developing reconstruction estimates in order to speed up the rebuilding process. We have been involved with site cleanup and environmental testing as well.” Allied Restoration’s office has been open as normal 24/7/365 during the wake of the fire disasters, and it will continue to be open for those in need. “We have staff available to review insurance estimates to ensure they are adequate rebuild requirements in our local commercial market,” Tabayoyon says. “We are equipped to write estimates on insurance carrier-approved pricing as well.” The company is committed to ensuring that the residents of Sonoma County and Santa Rosa City have local access to professional fire and smoke cleaning services that can facilitate their cleanup efforts. This will give fire victims a better opportunity to receive an objective and fair appraisal of the damages and help them determine what the actual costs would be from an independent point of view, according to Tabayoyon. It will also give people the benefit of having convenient and prompt access to fire and smoke cleaning services. Allied Restoration’s strong desire to assist victims of the catastrophic fires is consistent with its ongoing commitment for serving the community. The company, which has been operating in the San Francisco Bay Area for 20 years, has a longstanding practice of supporting the community during times of need. The enhancements that Allied Restoration has made to its resources are especially critical to mitigating the effects of the recent tragedy.

The October wildfires wreaked havoc on Lake, Mendocino, Napa and Sonoma counties. They burned an estimated 50,000 acres across Sonoma and Napa counties, consuming thousands of homes and businesses. Tragically, the fires claimed the lives of 44 people, including 22 residents of Sonoma County. In Santa Rosa—the county seat and gateway to the wine tourism industry—fires destroyed nearly 3,000 homes and caused $1.2 billion in damage. The affected communities are working together to complete cleanup efforts so people can rebuild their homes, businesses, and lives.

With the support of local companies like Allied Restoration, Sonoma County and Santa Rosa will continue to recover from the devastation of the fires.
