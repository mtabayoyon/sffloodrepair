7 Kitchen Remodeling Trends for 2015

Kitchen Remodeling Trends That Can Transform Your Home

Deciding to remodel your kitchen leads to many decisions about what will last for years and add value to your home. In 2015, form over function dominates kitchen design, as Martha Stewart explains. Streamlined spaces and a clean, seamless appearance replace kitchen clutter.

Garbage bins, refrigerators, dishwashers, toasters and other kitchen appliances now fit snuggly into modern cabinetry and disappear from sight, creating a less cluttered, more luxurious kitchen. Below are seven kitchen-remodeling trends for 2015 and beyond to help you make the right choices:

1. Wooden Kitchen Cabinets

Wood comes in a variety of colors, grains and stains, and the future is moving toward driftwood-inspired, mixed wood and blond-wood tones, such as white oak and gray.

2. Minimalist White Appliances and Kitchen Cabinets

White is the latest neutral, and it is uncluttered, simple and clean. It makes the kitchen appear bigger, especially if you blend appliances into cabinetry.

3. Gray and Metallic Kitchen Cabinetry

While gray is not a fresh trend, it continues to grow in popularity. Another neutral color trending this year, designers combine it with metallic surfaces and finishes to freshen tired kitchens.

4. Oil-Rubbed Bronze Finishes and Fixtures

Oil-rubbed bronze fixtures last into the future. These include knobs, faucets, dishcloth rings and handles, and the color complements marsala, Pantone’s color of the year.

5. Warm Metal Accents and Kitchen Fixtures

Brass and copper colors add warmth to a modern kitchen and create universal appeal. Designers combine warm metal colors with cool ones, which add visual interest, such as chrome and brass.

6. Colorful or White Sinks

Those embracing warm metals in their kitchens pair these fixtures with white sinks or harmonious warm colors. While they may complement stainless steel sinks, white offers a safer choice.

7. Open-Plan Kitchen Floors

Creating more space, or the illusion of it, makes the list of top trends. An open-plan theme in the kitchen and home achieves this, and it provides harmony.
