Basic Preparation

Next Steps

Unless a major hurricane or other storm is in the area, floods aren’t always easy to predict. Still, some areas are more prone to dangerous storms and flooding than others. As such, basic flood preparation often makes a huge difference for homeowners.

There are several steps people can take to prepare for floods that result from plumbing leaks. For example, it’s important to have cleaning supplies, such as a mop and towels, readily available. Other helpful supplies include rubber boots and gloves to keep dry when moving through the flooded area.

It’s also a smart idea to keep boots, gloves and other materials used around the flood in one area of the house to minimize the spread of water and other unwanted materials. This is especially important if the flood water is contaminated or involves backed-up sewage. Scheduling annual checkups on the condition of a home’s roof, plumbing and electricity is another way to avoid a disaster. Understanding how much time and money should go into flood prevention often hinges on knowing whether or not your area is considered a high-risk flood environment, according to Extension.

Preparing for Hurricanes and Heavy Storms

Major storm events often involve more comprehensive preparation as safety is a much more serious concern. Having the right supplies and paying attention to new developments as the storm progresses can often save lives.

Because major flooding can be the cause of serious power outages and other issues, having a supply of canned goods and fresh water is always important. Gas stoves do not require electricity to work, so heating water for food is still possible. For homes situated along the coasts and in areas prone to major storms and flooding, it might be a smart idea to invest in a backup power generator.

As the CDC advises, it is incredibly important to have a battery-powered radio and fresh batteries on hand to stay updated on storm progress in case of the need to evacuate.

As frightening as the notion of evacuation sounds, there are times when it’s necessary. Follow national broadcast instructions to their fullest, and make sure all gas and electricity is shut off before evacuating—provided it’s safe to do so.
