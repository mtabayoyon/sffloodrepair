Step-by-Step: Filing a Water Damage Insurance Claim

Step 1 — Stop the source

Shut off the water at the main valve. Turn off electricity in flooded areas. Your safety comes first — don't enter rooms with standing water and active electrical.

Step 2 — Document everything

Photograph and video every affected area before anything is moved or cleaned. Capture the source of damage, all affected rooms, furniture, flooring, and walls. Date and timestamp everything.

Step 3 — Call Allied Restoration

Call (415) 529-5637 before calling your insurer. We document damage with thermal imaging, moisture meters, and photographs — creating the professional documentation your claim requires. We also prevent further damage which protects your claim.

Step 4 — File your claim

Call your insurer's claims line or file online. Have your policy number ready. Tell them you have a restoration company on site already. Request a claim number — you'll need it for all future communications.

Step 5 — Meet your adjuster

Your insurer will assign an adjuster who visits the property to assess damage. Having Allied Restoration present at the adjuster meeting is critical — we know Xactimate pricing and can ensure the adjuster's scope matches the actual damage.

Step 6 — Review the estimate

The adjuster produces a Xactimate estimate. Allied Restoration reviews it against our own scope. If scope is missing (common) we file a supplemental claim. You should never accept a scope that doesn't fully cover the documented damage.

What Documentation to Gather

  • Photos and video of all damage — taken before any cleanup
  • Your insurance policy number and contact information
  • Source of loss documentation (plumber's report for burst pipe, etc.)
  • Inventory list of damaged personal property with estimated values
  • Any receipts for emergency expenses (hotel, temporary storage)
  • Allied Restoration's moisture logs and moisture mapping documentation
  • All contractor estimates and Xactimate reports

Questions to Ask Your Insurance Adjuster

  • What is the exact cause of loss you are covering?
  • What is my deductible for this type of claim?
  • Does my policy have a mold sublimit? (Many do — typically $5,000–$10,000)
  • Is my Additional Living Expense (ALE) coverage active? For how long?
  • Will you cover contents damage as well as structural damage?
  • What is the timeline for payment after the estimate is approved?
  • Do I need a contractor's estimate before you approve the scope?

What to Do If Your Claim Is Denied or Underpaid

  • Request the denial in writing with the specific policy language the adjuster is citing
  • File a supplemental claim if the adjuster's scope missed items — Allied Restoration can prepare supplemental Xactimate documentation
  • Invoke appraisal clause — most policies allow you to hire an independent appraiser if you dispute the valuation
  • Hire a public adjuster — a licensed public adjuster works for you (not the insurer) on a percentage fee
  • Contact California Department of Insurance at 800-927-4357 to file a complaint if you believe the claim is being handled in bad faith
