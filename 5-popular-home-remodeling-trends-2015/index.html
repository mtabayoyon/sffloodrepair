5 Popular Home Remodeling Trends for 2015

5 Popular Home Remodeling Trends for 2015

Professional interior designers have noticed a massive shift toward transitional concepts in their clients’ projects. This is a style that combines contemporary and traditional designs.

In San Francisco, there is a great emphasis on metal finishes, and homeowners are opting for clean, simple and clutter-free looks with quality air-filtration systems. People have said “goodbye” to formaldehyde finishes and rustic habitats.

According to Oregon Live, there are some home remodeling trends that are going to become evident in many properties throughout the Bay Area in 2015. The report predicts that the biggest trend will be creating a healthier living space with no-touch toilets and non-offgassing finishes.

Here are five trends that will take over San Francisco home remodeling over the next few months:

1. Advanced Creature Comforts 

Many creature comforts have enjoyed radical advancements in technology. More people are investing in heated towel racks, heated floors, heated swimming pools, rotating garages and other automatic products that improve their lifestyle.

2. Recycling 

Homeowners are recycling old products by reusing, reducing and giving them a complete rebirth. Sustainability experts are abuzz with the word “lifecycle” because some products never go out of fashion. For example, viridian wood can have a really rough look, but a little sanding and polishing reveals stunning colors and grain patterns.

3. Metal Finishes 

Metal finishes are becoming increasingly popular across San Francisco. Copper lighting, brass sinks and stainless-steel products blend beautifully together to create a wonderfully artistic design. Copper, in particular, offers a variety of stunning tones and shades that enhance the look of the home.

4. Colors 

San Francisco homeowners are moving away from single colors, replacing them with a mix of different hues. Greens and light-blues are dominating the interior decorating industry this year.

5. Contemporary Designs 

Interior designers across the country almost unanimously agree that there will be a major shift toward contemporary designs that offer clean simplicity. Homeowners are opting for serenity in their sanctuaries, preferring a soothing and relaxing atmosphere that is both beautiful and minimalistic.

San Francisco homeowners are always interested in property renovations, and interior design trends for 2015 are focusing on hygienic, modern and uncluttered homes that offer creature comforts and materials that blend well together.
