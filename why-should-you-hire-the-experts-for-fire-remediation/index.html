The Case for Professional Fire Remediation

If you just experienced a fire on your property, you are most likely confused and disoriented. One of the best steps you can take after a fire in your home is to contact a fire remediation expert and hire a professional. Here is why it matters more than you might think.

1. Smoke Damage Is Far More Dangerous Than It Looks

The visible effects of smoke — staining and odor — are just the surface of the problem. Smoke residue contains hundreds of toxic compounds including heavy metals, carcinogenic combustion byproducts, and acidic compounds that continue to damage surfaces and affect indoor air quality long after the fire. Attempting to clean smoke without proper protective equipment exposes you to these compounds directly.

2. Improper Cleaning Makes Smoke Damage Worse

Soot and smoke residue require specific cleaning techniques for each surface type. Using the wrong cleaning agent or scrubbing method can permanently set stains into surfaces that could otherwise be cleaned, spread contamination to unaffected areas, and drive particles deeper into porous materials. Professional technicians are trained in the specific protocols for each material type.

3. Hidden Damage Requires Professional Assessment

After a fire, structural damage may not be visible. Heat weakens structural members without burning them. Smoke penetrates wall cavities and settles in insulation. Water from firefighting saturates structural assemblies. A professional assessment identifies all of these hidden damage types and documents them for your insurance claim.

4. Insurance Claims Require Professional Documentation

Insurance carriers require professional documentation to process fire damage claims. This includes itemized Xactimate estimates, moisture readings, photo documentation, and scope of work reports. Attempting DIY cleanup before professional documentation can actually jeopardize your insurance claim by removing evidence of damage.

5. Odor Elimination Requires Industrial Equipment

Smoke odor cannot be eliminated with household products, candles, or air fresheners. Professional odor elimination uses ozone generators, hydroxyl machines, and thermal fogging to reach odor compounds in wall cavities, HVAC systems, and porous materials throughout the structure. This equipment is not available to consumers and requires trained operators.

6. Certification and Standards Matter

Allied Restoration Company is IICRC certified with FSRT (Fire and Smoke Restoration Technician) certified staff. This means our technicians have demonstrated technical competency and follow the IICRC S770 standard for fire and smoke restoration. Hiring a non-certified contractor risks substandard work that may not satisfy your insurance carrier.
