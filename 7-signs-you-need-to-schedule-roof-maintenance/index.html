7 Signs You Need to Schedule Roof Maintenance

Most homeowners only realize the need for a new roof once it starts leaking. Roof problems often cause leaks that require water-damage repair, but according to Angie’s List, it is not always necessary to replace the entire roof. Roof repairs occur frequently among homeowners in the Bay Area.

If you perform annual maintenance on your roof, it should survive the elements relatively unscathed. However, how do you know if roof repair in San Francisco will resolve some maintenance problems or if you need to reroof the house? Here are seven signs that your roof needs a checkup:

1. Age

Most roofs consist of asphalt shingles, which typically last 20 to 25 years. However, some factors may influence its life span, such as lack of sufficient ventilation. If there is only one layer of shingles, you may require roof repair, but if several layers exist on a 20-year-old installation, you may require a new roof.

2. Misshapen Shingles

Shingles buckle and curl over time, and misshapen ones may indicate the need for a new roof. Look for shingles that are losing granules or curling at the edges. Broken or loose shingles also signal the need for repair.

3. Roof Valleys

Roof valleys direct rain toward gutters and prevent water from pooling. Standing water can cause roofs to collapse from water damage. A compromised valley makes the roof susceptible to leaks, so look for damaged or missing shingles in these areas.

4. Missing Shingles

If your roof appears patchy because of missing shingles, Bob Vila says you need a new roof. Shingles fall out of position after years of sun exposure, rain, earthquakes and other natural elements.

5. Chimney Flashing

Chimney flashing can cause concern, especially if constructed from roof tar or cement, which may erode over time. Check for cracks and other insulation problems. You may need to replace old flashing with a watertight, long-term fitting made of metal.

6. Shingle Granules

As the lifecycle of your roof nears its end, the shingles begin losing granules. You can climb onto the roof to check, but it is easier to look in your gutters. Roof valleys direct these granules into the gutter system, where they accumulate and frequently cause gutter blockages.

7. Daylight

If you can see daylight peeking through your roof boards, it needs repair. Climb into your attic and check for light entering through the ceiling, and at the same time, test for moisture within the insulation system, as this indicates leaks and possible water damage from pooling.
