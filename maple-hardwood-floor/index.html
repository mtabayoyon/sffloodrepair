Homeowners concerned with finding the perfect flooring option to complement a recent renovation, or one that will work with a more traditional style in an older house, should consider maple hardwood floors. Tips on Caring for Maple Hardwood Floors

Maple is a low-maintenance wood. Even in high-traffic areas, maple only needs to be refinished every five to seven years. Little more than a broom or vacuum is necessary to prevent dirt and dust from eroding the floor. Due to its resistance to mold and bacteria, maple is very easy to keep sanitary. However, property owners should clean water spills as soon as possible. You should mop up liquids quickly to prevent damage to the finish and wood. It is always best to ask your contractor for advice regarding floor maintenance. Each floor is unique, and it takes professional knowledge to understand how the stain or finish affects the proper cleaning technique. Improper use of harsh chemicals can erode a finish and damage the flooring underneath. As with any hardwood floor, it is a smart idea to place felt pads under furniture. This can make moving tables and chairs easier, and reduce the chance of scratching the floorboards. Do not walk on a new hardwood floor for at least 48 hours. The finish needs time to dry.
