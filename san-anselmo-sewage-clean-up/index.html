Causes of Raw Sewage Leaks

Sewage backup can enter a homeowner's basement from multiple paths during storm conditions. It is the homeowner's responsibility to protect his or her property. An open floor drain and basement plumbing fixtures, including toilets, shower drains, sinks or laundry machines, are often the cause of the problem. When storm and sanitary sewers combine, it is essential to protect the home from sewage leaks. Since floor drains are supposed to draw water out of the basement, when a broken water pipe or rising groundwater cause flooding, wise homeowners will close them off. Open drains and basement fixtures expose your home and everyone in it to risks associated with sewer leaks. A professional sewage clean up service can ensure that drains and plumbing are capped off to prevent future sewage leaks or backups. Plant or tree growth can also cause sewage leaks. Over time, roots may grow within both lateral and blocking pipes that cause sewage backups that are unrelated to storm conditions. On rare occasions, a main sewer line may break or become overwhelmed by heavy rains. Sewage backups are likely to result in these conditions. Sewer Leak Emergency

Homeowners should keep professional quality rubber gloves and boots, breathing masks and protective goggles in preparation for a sewer backup emergency. Although property owners and inhabitants should never attempt DIY remediation, these items will protect people from foul-smelling waste and sewage pathogens. Large plastic sheets or tablecloths should be placed over door frames that lead to the sewage backup in order to reduce transmission of bacteria or organisms through the air. Move any people in poor or delicate health from the building until the sewage spill is cleaned. Inhabitants should not pour chlorine bleach onto the raw sewage because chlorine and the ammonia present in the wastewater can combine to create deadly fumes.

Harmful Bacteria, Organisms and Chemicals in Sewage

Raw sewage exposes humans to various harmful bacteria and organisms, including fecal coliforms, fecal streptococci, enterococci, Clostridium Perfringens, Stapholococcus, Pseudomonas, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, acid-fast bacteria, coliphages and bacteriodes. In addition, toxic chemicals, including hydrochloric acid, Stoddard's Solvent and pesticides, can cause dizziness, vomiting, sore throat, and eye or throat irritation. Pregnant women may be especially sensitive to these pathogens, so it is important to engage professional sewage clean up in San Anselmo.
