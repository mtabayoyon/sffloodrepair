Tile Floors That Add Style and Elegance Tile is a popular and versatile type of flooring that is suitable for a number of different commercial and residential applications. Tile flooring offers the opportunity to get creative and add style to your home or business. Tile floors are highly customizable with different shapes, textures, colors and materials. They are both durable and water resistant, and if a tile breaks, repairs are much easier—and less expensive—than other types of flooring, such as hardwood. Tile Flooring for Homes and Businesses in San Francisco Each tile flooring project is unique. There are several factors that influence the contractor’s approach, including the style of the interior, the purpose of the room, and whether the property is commercial or residential. Many people make the mistake of assuming that tiling is a DIY job, but inexperience will show in the final outcome. Messy grout-work and crooked tiles won’t create the impression you want. But a professional contractor from Allied Restoration Company can examine your property and recommend tile materials and styles that will bring the room to life. Choosing the Right Tiles for Your Home or Business Marble, Ceramic and Porcelain Tiles: These are popular among homeowners who really want to impress guests and add some aesthetic appeal to the interior. They are excellent for bathrooms with matching cabinetry, mirrors and other hardware. Stone Tiles: Stone tiles come in several varieties that complement different color schemes and styles. This choice is popular for walkways outside of a home or business. Linoleum Tiles: These are popular for kitchens in both homes and businesses. Linoleum tiles are naturally anti-bacterial, as well as highly durable, so they are great for high-traffic areas. Plastic and Rubber Tiles: These are particularly popular in commercial properties. They offer superior traction, and they are less likely to break. Homeowners also choose these tiles for garages, patios, pool decks and exercise areas. Floors with plastic or rubber tiles often feature a glue-free interlocking design. They are easy to install and work well in high-traffic areas that might require frequent flooring replacement.
