Industrial Desiccant Dehumidification for Large-Scale Projects

When a standard water loss requires advanced drying — or when a commercial property has suffered significant flooding — Allied Restoration deploys industrial desiccant dehumidifiers capable of processing up to 5,000 cubic feet of air per minute (CFM). This is the same class of equipment used in hospital restoration, large commercial buildings, and catastrophic flood events.

What Is a Desiccant Dehumidifier?

Unlike refrigerant dehumidifiers, which cool air to condense moisture, desiccant dehumidifiers use a silica gel or similar material to adsorb moisture directly from the air. This makes them effective at lower temperatures and capable of achieving much lower dew points — making them ideal for drying structural assemblies that refrigerant units cannot fully dry.

When We Deploy Desiccant Units

  • •Commercial properties with large floor plates
  • •Multi-story buildings with widespread moisture
  • •Cold conditions (below 65°F) where refrigerant units lose efficiency
  • •Crawl spaces, parking garages, and other hard-to-dry spaces
  • •Situations requiring very low humidity levels for specialty drying
  • •When temporary power is needed alongside drying equipment

Setup and Configuration

Deploying a 5,000 CFM desiccant unit requires careful planning. Ducting must be designed to direct dry air into the affected areas and exhaust humid air out of the building. We use psychrometric calculations to determine the ideal equipment placement, air exchange rates, and target dew point for each specific job. Daily monitoring and adjustment ensures the drying proceeds on schedule.

By the Numbers

A 5,000 CFM desiccant unit can process approximately 300,000 cubic feet of air per hour — equivalent to cycling the air volume of a 30,000 sq ft commercial building approximately 10 times per hour. This drying capacity enables us to tackle projects that would be impossible with residential-grade equipment.
