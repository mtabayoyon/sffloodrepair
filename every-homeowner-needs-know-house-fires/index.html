Common Causes of House Fires

Sometimes, the cause of a fire in the house is completely out of a homeowner's control. Being aware of the various causes, however, can help prevent one from ever occurring.

According to the National Fire Protection Association, the leading cause of most house fires is unattended cooking equipment. Heaters, intentional fires, electrical components and smoking also fall into the top five.

Smoke Damage: Worse Than Flames

According to many restoration and fire authorities, smoke poses risks quite possibly worse than the originating flames. While most homeowners are familiar with the health risks associated with breathing in smoke, the  amage capable from the leftover residue frequently goes unnoticed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Smoke does leave a familiar smell and odor, but that's not the only threat. As an acidic substance, smoke residue can easily eat through and destroy most property in the home, according to the Chicora Foundation. The longer smoke remains, the harder it is to reverse the damage.

In general, smoke contributes to unhealthy living conditions as it leaves a toxic residue. Also, its ability to destroy property long after extinguishing the fire can create the conditions for new flames to spark.
