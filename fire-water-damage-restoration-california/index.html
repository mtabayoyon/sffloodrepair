1. Smoke Damage

Fire creates smoke and that could cause trouble for your home, furnishings and personal possessions. Almost every item in your home could fall victim to smoke damage—from the upholstery to your furniture, clothing, wall hangings, window treatments and carpeting.

Because smoke residue is often sticky and oily, it can be extremely difficult to remove. Smoke residue also gives off an unpleasant lingering odor, which can coat fabrics and other materials in your home.

2. Water Damage

Frequently Asked Questions

It’s no secret that water extinguishes fire, but what may not be so obvious while firefighters are fighting the flames is that water can actually pose additional risks to your home and belongings. Water damage can lead to bacterial growth, musty odors and the proliferation of mold. Water damage can also destroy fragile materials like fabric and paper.

3. Mold Growth

If left unchecked, water damage can lead to mold. Mold grows in dark and damp areas, and can be difficult to discover at first. Wood, drywall and your home’s ventilation system are susceptible to mold growth.

In addition, mold gives off an unsightly appearance and unpleasant odor. Mold is also hazardous to your health. Toxic black mold can cause respiratory problems and even death. This is why it is so important to hire an experienced professional to remove mold.
