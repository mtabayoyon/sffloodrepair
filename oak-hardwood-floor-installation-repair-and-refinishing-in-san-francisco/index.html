Oak Hardwood Floors Are Easy to Maintain

Hardwood floors not only add an extra level of beauty and luxury to a home or place of business, but they also are relatively easy to maintain. However, if you want your floors to keep their luster through the years, it is important to know how to clean them properly. It is best to avoid harsh chemicals—especially on new floors—as this can damage the finish. You can use a broom or vacuum to clean dirt and dust. If you use a vacuum, add a hose attachment to avoid scratches and scuffmarks. After a new installation, it is always a smart idea to ask the contractor about specific ways to clean and care for the flooring. Different woods, stains and finishes may have certain restrictions when it comes to using soaps and cleaners. Do not walk on newly finished oak hardwood floors for at least two days after installation. Also, try to keep traffic to a minimum for at least a month. This allows the finish to dry, undisturbed, and can lengthen the lifespan of the floor.
