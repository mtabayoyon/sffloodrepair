What Mold Is and Why It Requires Immediate Removal

All About Mold Testing: What Mold Is and Why It Requires Immediate Removal

Mold is a fungus that grows in moist, dark areas. It can spread quickly by releasing spores into the air.

For homeowners, mold can be an absolute nightmare. It often grows in basements or attics, which many property owners seldom visit. Some mistakenly assume that mold and its characteristic odor are normal conditions for attics and basements. The truth, however, is that mold is simply too dangerous to leave unattended. When mold has time to grow, a massive outbreak may result, which not only can damage property but can also pose health risks to building inhabitants.

Mold Never Stays Confined to Attics and Basements 

Because many assume that mold growth is a problem exclusive to basements and attics, ignoring it is quite easy. What many are not aware of, however, is that mold continuously releases spores into the air, and these can easily reach high-traffic areas, especially in homes with HVAC systems. Kitchens and bathrooms are prone to mold growth. 

According to the EPA, mold can cause persistent allergic reactions. Because the active sites will release spores regularly, allergic reactions can happen throughout the day and during any season. They generally will worsen until removing the mold. 

Mold Presents Serious Risks to Health 

Those who suffer from asthma are likely to experience the most severe reactions to mold. People can easily inhale airborne spores, which can cause violent asthmatic attacks. In severe cases, victims may end up in the hospital.

Recent studies show links between exposure to mold and respiratory illnesses later in life. This is of particular concern for children because their bodies are continuing to develop.

Mold Threatens More Than Just Breathing Conditions 

While mold is very dangerous for children, the elderly, asthmatics and allergy sufferers when it comes to health, spores can also irritate the skin, eyes and throat in fit individuals. 

Mold growth also threatens property. It can easily destroy wood, paper, clothing and almost any other substance.
